# 02. Horizons: from six months to ten years

[← Index](../README.md) · [← 01 State of play](01-state-of-play.md)

The right question is not "is Europe sovereign?" but "when, and over what?". A snapshot always lies, because the technology keeps moving; a trajectory tells the truth. Here, then, is the realistic state of sovereignty, horizon by horizon, dated, with no rhetorical optimism. The conclusion fits in a single sentence, which we then prove: the sovereign bet will be won or lost in the next twenty-four to thirty-six months, not in 2036.

---

## Six months (end of 2026), Zero sovereignty upstream, partial sovereignty in use

The tipping point has already passed. On 12 June 2026, a model deployed to hundreds of millions of people was shut down worldwide within hours by US decree. The first forced takedown by a state of a commercial frontier model. At this horizon, the only asset Europe can convert immediately into sovereignty is open weights (Mistral 3 under a free license, runnable offline, immune to a shutdown) backed by France's low-carbon nuclear power. Everything else is declaratory: Europe accounts for roughly 5% of global compute, its only exascale supercomputer runs 100% on Nvidia GPUs, no gigafactory is funded, and the political response to the Fable affair is unanimous but purely verbal. ASML remains the global chokepoint that Europe holds without being able to exploit it.

**Verdict: exposed and dependent, response is declaratory.**

## One year (mid-2027), Use consolidates, the upstream stays empty

TSMC's ESMC foundry in Dresden goes into production, but on nodes of 28 to 16 nanometres, two generations below the frontier: zero leading-edge gain. The first EuroHPC gigafactories may be shortlisted, but grid connection caps everything, and the promised megawatts are not deliverable before 2028. Mistral brings its Essonne data center online. The French presidential election turns AI sovereignty into a cross-party pillar, and the Cloud and AI Development Act enters negotiation. But the cloud response (the AWS European Sovereign Cloud launched in January 2026) does not legally neutralize the CLOUD Act: as long as the entity remains under US control, the exposure persists.

**Verdict: growing autonomy in use and regulation, physical dependence unchanged.**

## Three years (2029), Partial sovereignty, capped

Europe can reach autonomy in use, law and energy, plus a credible open sub-frontier. It remains structurally dependent for leading-edge chips, HBM memory, materials and high-end compute. None of the hard bottlenecks is resolved, at best under construction. The world fragments into three technology stacks (American, Chinese, a European attempt), what the philosopher Yuk Hui calls technodiversity. And the Taiwan risk overshadows everything: a shock on the island would collapse the global chip chain, with no European substitute.

**Verdict: sovereignty in use achieved, material sovereignty out of reach.**

## Five years (2031), Real sovereignty possible, but decided now

Two scenarios diverge. Either Europe has a credible frontier champion (thanks to sovereign compute and accumulated capital), or the capital and compute gap has frozen it into a second-tier power. The determining factor does not lie five years out: it is the energized compute between 2027 and 2029, hence decided today, in the grid-connection queues we join now. At this horizon, China will probably have demonstrated functional compute autonomy under sanctions, an embarrassing mirror: proof that autonomy is achievable in five years with a state will that Europe lacks.

**Verdict: functional sovereignty plausible over use and energy, leading-edge material sovereignty still missing.**

## Ten years (2036), The only horizon for complete sovereignty, entirely conditional

This is the only horizon where a European leading-edge foundry is physically conceivable. And the blocker is not access to EUV, since Europe owns ASML: the blocker is capital, the ecosystem, HBM, materials, process know-how. If the major plans (EuroStack, Chips Act 2.0) are executed without interruption across three political cycles, a bet historically rarely held in Europe, then Europe could fabricate within one or two generations of the frontier. Honest verdict: possible, not probable at the pace and fragmentation observed in 2026.

**Verdict: the only window for complete material sovereignty, and it depends entirely on the decisions of 2026 to 2029.**

---

## Summary of the horizons

| Horizon | Sovereignty verdict |
|---------|---------------------|
| 6 months | Zero upstream, partial in use. Declaratory response. |
| 1 year | Use and law consolidate, upstream still zero. |
| 3 years | Sovereignty in use achieved, material sovereignty out of reach. |
| 5 years | Real sovereignty possible but not guaranteed, decided in 2026-2027. |
| 10 years | The only window for complete sovereignty, entirely conditional. |

The trap of the ten-year horizon is to believe there is time. There is not. Everything that will be true in 2031 depends on the grid-connection queues we join and the foundries we decide on in 2026 and 2027. The clock is the real subject.
